# TandPay Social Media Strategy — Algorithm & Virality Playbook

## How the algorithms work (simplified)

Every platform ranks your post based on the same core signals:

1. **Early engagement** — First 30-60 minutes decide everything. If people like/comment/share quickly, the algorithm pushes it to more people
2. **Dwell time** — How long people stop scrolling to read your post
3. **Saves & shares** — Worth 5-10x more than likes (signals "this is valuable")
4. **Completion rate** (TikTok/Reels) — If people watch your video to the end, it gets pushed hard

**Consistency matters more than volume.** Posting 4x/week every week beats posting 20x one week then disappearing.

## What actually makes posts go viral

### 1. Lead with pain, not your product
- Bad: "TandPay is an escrow platform..."
- Good: "A seller on WhatsApp just took my friend's 30,000 RWF and disappeared. Here's how to never let this happen to you..."

### 2. Use local stories and numbers
- "A student in Kigali lost 45,000 RWF..."
- "8 million Rwandans use MoMo but zero have buyer protection"
- Real stories from real people >>> statistics from reports

### 3. The "save this" format
Posts that people save get pushed 10x more. Structure posts as:
- "5 signs a seller is scamming you"
- "7 tips before you send MoMo to a stranger"
- "Save this checklist before your next online purchase"

### 4. Engagement bait that actually works
- Ask questions: "What's the most money you've lost to an online scammer? (no judgment)"
- Polls: "Would you send MoMo to a seller you've never met? Yes/No"
- "Tag someone who needs to see this"

### 5. The 80/20 rule
- **80% value** — tips, stories, education, entertainment
- **20% promotion** — "use TandPay"
- If every post is "use TandPay," people unfollow

## TikTok specifically (your biggest opportunity)

TikTok is the ONE platform where a brand new account can go viral on day one. The algorithm doesn't care about follower count — only content quality.

**Video ideas that could blow up in Rwanda:**
- "I sent 50,000 RWF to 5 Instagram sellers. Here's what happened." (scam experiment)
- "POV: You just got scammed on WhatsApp" (relatable skit)
- "How to check if a seller is legit in 30 seconds"
- "I asked Kigali street vendors if they'd use escrow" (man-on-street interviews)
- "What is escrow? Explained in Kinyarwanda in 60 seconds"

**TikTok hack:** Post the SAME content as Reels on Instagram and Shorts on YouTube. One video, three platforms.

## The real secret

The posts that go viral aren't about your product. They're about **the problem your product solves.** Talk about scams, trust, lost money, frustration — and people will find TandPay on their own.

Your unfair advantage: **Nobody in Rwanda is creating content about safe online shopping.** You own that entire niche right now. First mover wins.

Start posting today. Even if it's ugly. Consistency + local relevance beats perfection every time.
