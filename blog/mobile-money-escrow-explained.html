Mobile Money Escrow: How TandPay Protects Buyers and Sellers

By Irene Bahati · March 4, 2026 · 8 min read

You find a beautiful dress on Instagram. The seller says "Send money to my MoMo, I'll deliver tomorrow." You send 25,000 RWF. Tomorrow comes. No dress. No reply. Your money is gone.

Or maybe you are the seller. You delivered the shoes. The buyer says "They don't fit" and demands a refund -- after wearing them for a week. You are stuck.

These stories happen every single day in Rwanda. With over 7 million MTN Mobile Money accounts in the country, MoMo has become the way we pay for everything. But when it comes to buying and selling on WhatsApp, Instagram, or TikTok, there is a massive gap: MoMo was built for transfers, not for commerce.

That is exactly the problem mobile money escrow solves. And TandPay is the first platform in Rwanda to bring this solution to social commerce.

What Is Mobile Money Escrow?

Let us break it down simply. You already know what MTN MoMo is -- you use it to send money, pay bills, and buy airtime. And you may have heard the word "escrow" before, maybe in real estate or international trade. Escrow just means: a trusted third party holds money until both sides are happy.

Mobile money escrow combines these two ideas into a digital escrow system built for mobile money protection. Instead of sending money directly to a seller (and hoping for the best), your payment goes through a secure platform that holds the funds until the product is delivered and you confirm you received it.

Think of it like this

Imagine you are buying something at a local market, and instead of handing cash directly to the seller, you give it to a trusted market official standing right there. The official holds your money. The seller shows you the product, you inspect it, you say "yes, this is what I ordered." Only then does the official hand your money to the seller. That is escrow -- and TandPay does this digitally with MTN MoMo.

Why Traditional MoMo Transfers Are Risky for Commerce

MTN Mobile Money is an incredible tool. It has brought financial inclusion to millions of Rwandans. But it was designed for person-to-person transfers, not for buying and selling products between strangers online.

Here is the problem: when you send money via MoMo, it is an instant, irreversible transfer. The moment you confirm that USSD prompt, your money is in the other person's account. There is no "undo" button. There is no dispute process built into MoMo itself.

This creates risk on both sides, and highlights the urgent need for buyer protection in Rwanda and seller protection in Rwanda alike:

  • For buyers: You pay first and hope the seller is honest. If they do not deliver, or deliver something different from what was promised, you have very limited recourse. You can report to the police, but recovering 15,000 RWF through legal channels is not realistic.
  • For sellers: If you deliver first and the buyer decides not to pay, you lose both the product and the revenue. And if a buyer claims they never received the item, it becomes a "he said, she said" situation with no proof.

The TandPay Flow: Step by Step

So how does TandPay actually work? Let us walk through a real transaction from start to finish. It is simpler than you might think.

Step 1: Seller creates an order or product listing

As a seller, you log into TandPay and create a listing for your product. You add a title, description, price, and photos. Or, if you are doing a custom deal, you create a one-time order with the buyer's phone number and amount. It takes less than a minute.

Step 2: Seller shares the payment link

TandPay generates a unique payment link for the order. You copy this link and share it with your buyer -- on WhatsApp, Instagram DMs, TikTok comments, or anywhere else you sell. The link works on any phone, no app needed.

Step 3: Buyer opens the link

When the buyer clicks the link, they see the product details, the seller's profile, trust score, and reviews from previous buyers. This builds confidence before they even think about paying. The buyer does not need a TandPay account -- they just need a phone number and an MTN MoMo account.

Step 4: Buyer pays via MoMo

The buyer enters their phone number and confirms the payment. They receive an MTN MoMo USSD prompt on their phone -- the same prompt they are used to for any MoMo transaction. They enter their MoMo PIN, and the payment is processed.

Important: The money does NOT go to the seller

This is the key difference. When the buyer pays, the money is held securely in TandPay's escrow. The seller can see that the payment has been made, but cannot access the funds yet. The buyer's money is safe.

Step 5: Seller delivers the product

Now the seller knows the buyer has paid -- so they can deliver the product with confidence. Whether it is same-day delivery in Kigali or shipping to another province, the seller delivers the item along with a unique 4-digit delivery code that TandPay generates for this specific order.

Step 6: Buyer confirms delivery with the code

When the buyer receives the product and is satisfied, they enter the 4-digit delivery code. This is their way of saying: "Yes, I received exactly what I ordered." The code is unique to each transaction and cannot be guessed or reused.

Step 7: Funds released to the seller

The moment the buyer enters the correct delivery code, TandPay automatically releases the funds to the seller's MoMo account, minus the platform fee. The transaction is complete. Both sides are happy.

The whole process in one sentence

Buyer pays, money is held safely, seller delivers, buyer confirms with a code, seller gets paid. Simple, safe, and fair for everyone.

What Happens If There Is a Dispute?

Life is not always perfect. Sometimes a buyer receives a product that is damaged or different from what was described. Sometimes a seller delivers but the buyer claims they did not receive it. TandPay has a clear process for these situations.

After delivery, the buyer has a 12-hour window to report an issue. If they do, the transaction is flagged and a TandPay admin reviews it. Here is what happens:

  1. Both sides submit evidence. The buyer explains the issue and can upload photos. The seller can respond with their own evidence (delivery photos, chat screenshots, etc.).
  2. Admin reviews the case. A TandPay team member examines the evidence from both sides, reviews the order details, and contacts both parties if needed.
  3. Resolution is issued. Depending on the evidence, the admin either releases the funds to the seller (if the complaint is not valid) or refunds the buyer (if the product was genuinely not as described or not delivered).

This process protects both buyers and sellers, keeping every MoMo payment safe and accountable. Buyers know they can get their money back if something goes wrong. Sellers know they will not lose money to false claims because evidence is required.

Security You Can Trust

When money is involved, security is not optional. Here is how TandPay keeps your data and funds safe:

  • End-to-end encryption: All data transmitted between your phone and TandPay's servers is encrypted using industry-standard TLS.
  • PIN hashing: Your login PIN is never stored in plain text. It is hashed using cryptographic algorithms, meaning even TandPay's own team cannot see your PIN.
  • Row-Level Security (RLS): The database enforces strict access controls. Sellers can only see their own data. No one can access another user's transactions or personal information.
  • NCSA compliance: TandPay is registered with Rwanda's National Cyber Security Authority as a data controller, following all data protection regulations under Rwandan law.
  • MTN MoMo API integration: Payments go through MTN's official API -- the same infrastructure that processes millions of MoMo transactions daily.
