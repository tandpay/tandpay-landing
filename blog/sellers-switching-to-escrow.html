Why Sellers in Kigali Are Switching to Escrow Payments

By Irene Bahati · February 16, 2026 · 9 min read

If you sell online in Kigali -- whether it is clothes on Instagram, electronics on WhatsApp, or beauty products through TikTok -- you already know the drill. You post your product. Someone messages you. You negotiate the price. And then comes the awkward part: "Send money to my MoMo."

Sometimes the buyer sends the money and you deliver happily. But other times? The buyer ghosts you. Or they receive the product and then message you saying "it is not what I expected" and demand a refund -- after clearly using it. Or they claim they never received the delivery at all, even though you handed it to them in person.

If any of this sounds familiar, you are not alone. These are the biggest headaches of selling online in Rwanda. And they are exactly why more and more sellers in Kigali are switching to escrow payments through TandPay.

The Seller's Problem Nobody Talks About

Everyone talks about protecting buyers. "Don't get scammed." "Don't send money to strangers." And yes, buyer protection is important. But here is something people forget: seller protection in Rwanda matters just as much -- because sellers get scammed too.

As a seller, you face risks that buyers never think about:

  • Buyers who ghost after you deliver. You sent the product on good faith. The buyer received it. Then they stop replying. If you delivered before getting paid, you just lost your product and your revenue.
  • Fake "not received" claims. You delivered the shoes to the buyer's door. Two days later, they message you: "I never received anything." It is your word against theirs, and you have no proof.
  • Refund requests after use. The buyer wears the dress to an event, takes photos for Instagram, then messages you: "It does not fit properly, I want a refund." You are stuck between losing a customer and losing money on a product that is no longer new.
  • "Send money first" arguments. Every transaction starts with a trust negotiation. The buyer wants you to deliver first. You want them to pay first. Neither side wants to take the risk. Sometimes the deal falls apart entirely because nobody will go first.

The real cost of distrust

Every deal that falls apart because of a trust argument is lost revenue. Every hour you spend chasing a buyer for payment is time you could be selling to someone else. And every bad experience makes you think twice about selling online at all. Distrust is not just frustrating -- it is expensive.

Trust Is a Two-Way Street

Here is the truth that most people miss: in social commerce, trust has to work in both directions. The buyer needs to trust that you will deliver what you promised. But you also need to trust that the buyer will pay you -- and that they will not make a false claim after receiving the product.

When trust only goes one way, someone always gets hurt. Direct MoMo transfers put all the trust burden on one side. Either the buyer trusts the seller enough to pay first, or the seller trusts the buyer enough to deliver first. Someone is always taking a risk.

Escrow eliminates this problem entirely. With an escrow marketplace like TandPay, neither side has to trust the other blindly. The money is held by a neutral third party. The seller delivers knowing they will get paid. The buyer pays knowing their money is protected. Trust is built into the system, making it easy to buy and sell in Rwanda with confidence.

What escrow means for you as a seller

When a buyer pays through TandPay, the money is held securely in escrow -- not in the buyer's account, and not in yours yet. You can see the payment has been made. You deliver the product with a 4-digit delivery code. The buyer enters the code to confirm they received it. Your money is released. No arguments, no risk, no chasing.

Real Talk: What About the 12-Hour Issue Window?

Some sellers worry about the 12-hour issue reporting window. After delivery, the buyer has 12 hours to report a problem. "What if they abuse it?" Fair question.

Here is why it actually protects you:

  • 12 hours is short. It is enough time for a buyer to inspect what they received, but not enough time to use and abuse the product. A buyer cannot wear your dress to a wedding, decide they do not want it, and file a complaint the next week.
  • Evidence is required. If a buyer files a dispute, they need to provide evidence -- photos, descriptions, proof that the product is not as described. "I just changed my mind" is not a valid dispute.
  • You can respond. You will be notified of any dispute, and you can submit your own evidence. If you delivered the correct product in good condition, you have nothing to worry about.
  • Admin decides fairly. A TandPay admin reviews both sides and makes a decision based on evidence, not just the buyer's word. Sellers are protected from false claims.

After the 12-hour window closes with no issue reported, your funds are released and the transaction is final. No buyer can come back a week later with a complaint. This protects you far more than direct MoMo transfers, where a buyer can hassle you for a refund indefinitely.
